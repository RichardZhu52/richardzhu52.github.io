I am a graduating senior at **Harvard University**, completing a joint S.M. in Computer Science and A.B. in Statistics (Bioinformatics and Computational Biology track) in May 2026, with a 4.00 GPA. I am an **NSF Graduate Research Fellowship (GRFP) Awardee** and have been recognized as a Rhodes Scholarship Finalist, Marshall Scholarship Finalist, and John Harvard Scholar (top 5% of class).

## Research Interests

My research focuses on developing **AI methods for accelerating drug discovery**, with a particular emphasis on patients with few or no treatment options — including rare disease patients. I am interested in building **multimodal AI models** that integrate information across biochemistry, structural biology, cell biology, and medicine to generate research hypotheses.

A central goal of my work is developing computational tools for designing **therapeutic biomolecules** (proteins, small molecules, and other biologics) informed by chemical, biological, and clinical constraints. I am especially interested in problems at the intersection of:

- **Structural biology & protein modeling** — docking, binding affinity prediction, protein design
- **Therapeutic reasoning & clinical AI** — agents that reason across biological and medical knowledge
- **Drug discovery pipelines** — target identification, molecular optimization, ADMET prediction

## Current Work

I am currently a Machine Learning Research Intern in **Dr. Marinka Zitnik's Lab at Harvard Medical School**, where I have worked on [TxAgent](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2503.10970), an AI agent for therapeutic reasoning, and [ToolUniverse](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2509.23426), an ecosystem of 600+ tools for AI scientists. I am also co-organizing **CURE-Bench**, an international NeurIPS competition benchmarking AI models on therapeutic reasoning.

I completed an NSF Rosetta Commons REU at **Johns Hopkins University (Dr. Jeffrey Gray's Lab)**, where I fine-tuned DFMDock — a diffusion model for protein-protein docking — with a learning-to-rank objective, outperforming state-of-the-art scoring methods.

## Background

Before focusing on ML for drug discovery, I worked in computational clinical research at **Massachusetts General Hospital** on hand-function biomarkers, molecular biology at **Brigham and Women's Hospital** on Alzheimer's disease mechanisms, and bioinformatics at **UCLA** studying mammalian brain aging.

Outside research, I have served as a Teaching Fellow and Course Assistant for courses in algorithms, stochastic processes, and statistics at Harvard, and I lead the Harvard Quadball Club (2024 Division I National Champions).
